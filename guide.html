What Is This?

Family Bookshelf is a private web app for tracking your reading across three lists, connecting with friends and family, and discovering new books. It works in any browser and can be installed as an app on your phone. Your data is stored securely in the cloud and syncs across all devices.

Accounts & Sign In

Creating an Account

  1. Go to bookshelf.mcbrid3.com and tap Sign Up.
  2. Enter your name, email address, and a password (8+ characters).
  3. Tap Create Account — you're logged in immediately.
  4. A short onboarding walkthrough introduces the main features. Tap through it or skip.
Each family member needs their own account. Book lists are private — no one sees your lists unless you connect as friends.

Signing In

  1. Go to the app URL.
  2. Enter your email and password, tap Sign In.
  3. If MFA is enabled, enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator app.

Two-Factor Authentication (MFA)

MFA requires a time-based code from your phone in addition to your password. Strongly recommended for all accounts.

Setting Up MFA

  1. Tap your avatar (top-right corner) to open your profile.
  2. Find Two-Factor Auth (MFA) and tap Set up.
  3. A QR code appears. Open Google Authenticator or Authy on your phone.
  4. Tap + → Scan QR code. Scan the code on screen.
  5. Enter the 6-digit code shown in your authenticator app, then tap Confirm & Enable MFA.
Important: TOTP codes expire every 30 seconds. If a code is rejected, wait for it to refresh and try the new one.

Removing MFA

Open your profile, tap Remove next to MFA status, and confirm.

Searching for Books

  1. Tap the Search tab at the bottom of the screen.
  2. Type a title, author name, or ISBN. Tap the ✕ button inside the field to clear it.
  3. Press Enter or tap Search.
  4. Results appear. The app checks its local cache first to reduce API usage; if fewer than 5 matches are cached it falls through to Google Books.
  5. Tap a result to open the book detail sheet, or tap + Add to list directly on the card.
Books already in one of your lists show a checkmark next to that list — prevents accidental duplicates.

Barcode Scanner

Tap the barcode icon next to the search bar to open the camera scanner. Hold a book's back cover up to the camera. The app reads the ISBN and searches automatically. Requires camera permission.

Adding a Book Not Found in Search

If a book can't be found, scroll to the bottom of a list and tap Add book manually. Fill in the title, author, page count, description, and optionally upload a cover photo. Manual books are saved to the shared book cache so they can be found in future searches.

Managing Your Books

View Modes

Each list has three view modes, toggled by the icons in the list header:

  • List view — book cards with cover, title, author, and action buttons
  • Cover grid — compact grid of cover thumbnails
  • Bookshelf spine — horizontal carousel styled as a bookshelf

Book Detail Sheet

Tap any book cover or title to open the full detail sheet. See the Book Detail Sheet section for everything available there.

Book Detail Sheet

Open by tapping any book cover or title. Everything about a book is in one place here.

For books in your lists

  • Star rating — tap 1–5 stars
  • Reading progress — enter current page / total pages (Purchased only)
  • Notes — free-text note, tap Save note
  • Series — series name and book number, auto-detected where possible
  • Update cover — tap the pencil icon on the cover to upload a custom photo (your change only, doesn't affect other users)
  • Mark as reading — flags as Currently Reading (Purchased only)
  • Shop for this book — Google search in a new tab
  • Recommend to friend — send to a connected friend
  • Share book — copies a link or opens the native share sheet
  • Move to… — move to another list
  • Remove from list

For search results (not yet in a list)

  • Description, author, year
  • Add to To Read / Purchased / Read
  • Shop for this book
  • Recommend to friend
  • Share book
Custom cover: The pencil icon only appears on books already in one of your lists. Upload any image — it applies only to your account.

Series Tracking

The app can track which series a book belongs to and what number it is in that series.

  • Open a book's detail sheet. A Series section appears below the description.
  • The app attempts to auto-detect the series from the book title (e.g. "Dune (Dune Chronicles, #1)") and from Open Library data.
  • If auto-detection finds something, a toast notification appears. Tap outside the field to save.
  • You can always edit the series name and number manually — tap outside to save.
  • Other books from the same series that are in your library appear at the bottom of the detail sheet.

Importing & Exporting

Importing from a Spreadsheet

Bulk-add books from a CSV file instead of searching one by one.

  1. Go to the list you want to import into.
  2. Tap the upload icon next to the book count badge.
  3. Upload a .csv or .txt file, or paste data directly.
  4. Your file needs two columns: Book Title and Author.
  5. Tap Import Books. Progress is shown for each entry.
Large imports (50+ books) take a minute or two. The app adds a small delay between lookups to stay within Google Books rate limits. Books that can't be found are still saved with the title/author you provided.

Exporting a List

Tap the download icon next to the book count badge on any list. A CSV file downloads immediately containing title, author, rating, page count, finish date, and description for every book in that list.

Friends & Social

The Friends tab lets you connect with other users, see their reading activity, and exchange recommendations.

Connecting with a Friend

  1. Ask the other person for their Friend Code (found in their profile panel — 6 characters, e.g. AB3XY7).
  2. On the Friends tab, tap Connect with friend and enter the code.
  3. The connection is bidirectional — both users immediately see each other's activity.

Recommending a Book

  1. Open the book's detail sheet (from your lists or search results).
  2. Tap Recommend to friend.
  3. Pick a friend from the list and optionally add a note.
  4. Tap Send recommendation.

The recipient sees it in their Recommended for You section on the Friends tab with a New badge.

Disconnecting a Friend

On the Friends tab, tap the Disconnect button next to a friend's name. This removes the connection for both users.

Book Clubs

Book clubs let a group read the same book together and track shared progress.

Creating a Club

  1. On the Friends tab, scroll to Book Clubs and tap Create Club.
  2. Enter a club name and tap Create.
  3. Your club appears with a join code others can use.
  4. Optionally set a Current Book — search for a book and select it.

Joining a Club

  1. Get the club's join code from the club creator.
  2. On the Friends tab, tap Join Club, enter the code, and tap Join.

Stats Tab

The Stats tab gives you an overview of your reading history and goals.

  • Library counts — total books in each list at a glance
  • Currently Reading — books marked as reading now with progress bars
  • Yearly reading goal — set a target number of books for the year; a progress bar tracks how close you are
  • Books per year — bar chart of how many books you finished each year
  • Top authors — authors who appear most in your Read list

New Releases

Scroll down on the Search tab to find the New Releases section. Tap a genre tab to load recent books in that category: Fiction, Non-Fiction, Mystery, Sci-Fi, Romance, Fantasy, Biography, or Thriller.

Results are cached after the first load — switching tabs is instant. Tap any book to open the detail sheet and add it to a list.

Sharing a Book

Any book can be shared with anyone — even people who don't have an account yet.

  1. Open the book's detail sheet.
  2. Tap Share book.
  3. On mobile, the native share sheet opens (Messages, WhatsApp, etc.). On desktop, the link is copied to your clipboard.
  4. The recipient opens the link, signs in (or creates an account), and the book detail opens automatically.

Installing on Your Phone (Home Screen App)

iPhone (Safari)

  1. Open the app in Safari (must be Safari, not Chrome).
  2. Tap the Share button (box with arrow pointing up).
  3. Tap Add to Home Screen.
  4. Tap Add.

Android (Chrome)

  1. Open the app in Chrome.
  2. Tap the three-dot menu (top right).
  3. Tap Add to Home screen.
  4. Tap Add.
Once installed, the app opens full-screen like a native app with no browser address bar.

Troubleshooting

ProblemSolution
MFA code says "Incorrect"TOTP codes are time-sensitive. Make sure your phone's clock is on automatic. Wait for the code to refresh (every 30 seconds) and try the new one.
Stuck on "Verifying…" during MFATap ← Back to sign in, sign in again with email & password, then try the MFA code again.
Book not found in searchTry title only, or author only. For obscure/self-published books, use Add book manually.
"Search limit reached"The app hit the daily Google Books anonymous quota. It resets at midnight Pacific time. Cached books still work.
Friend code "not found"Double-check the code — it's case-sensitive. Ask the friend to verify their code from their profile panel.
Friend's books not showingMake sure the connection is bidirectional — both users should see each other listed. Try disconnecting and reconnecting.
Custom cover not appearingThe pencil icon only appears on books already in your list (not search results). Make sure the book is in a list first.
Series not auto-detectedAuto-detection works on common title patterns like "(Series Name, #1)". For other formats, enter the series name manually.
Import shows many failuresCheck column headers are exactly Book Title and Author (row 1). Remove special characters or extra columns.
Changes not showing upThe app may be serving a cached version. Force-close and reopen, or pull down to refresh.
Forgot passwordContact the person managing the Supabase project to reset it manually.
